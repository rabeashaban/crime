Hacking crime

Information networks have become the basis on which all organizations and bodies, whether governmental or private, around the world depend, and even countries themselves have represented the information network and the basic symptoms in collecting and preserving all the information that pertains to its readers and dealing with it with other countries, as well as the state’s economic, political, and military secrets. And so in parallel with this global system. This global system is characterized by the fame of many people who call themselves “hackers” or pirates, and their experience in penetrating these information networks, whether for or for global bodies and institutions, and the accounts of specific parties or their personal accounts for the purpose of monitoring, blackmailing, espionage, and voyeurism. Accordingly, the legislator established a package of laws and legislation that would punish these electronic gangs, as required by the law and in accordance with each incident committed, as the Anti-Information Technology Crimes Law No. 175 of 2018 specified the penalty for attempting to commit a crime from the crimes stipulated in the law, which is half. The penalty prescribed for the crime. Article 40 stipulates that anyone who attempts to commit the misdemeanors stipulated in this law shall be punished not to exceed half the maximum penalty prescribed for the crime.

In this regard, Alaa Mukhtar, the lawyer, explains that the law sets penalties for the crime of attacking the integrity of the information network, as Article 21 stipulates that he shall be punished by imprisonment for a period of not less than six months, and a fine of not less than one hundred thousand pounds and not exceeding five hundred thousand pounds, or one of these two. The two penalties include anyone who intentionally causes an information network to stop working, disrupts it, limits the efficiency of its work, disturbs it, impedes it, intercepts its work, or unlawfully performs electronic processing of its data.

Mukhtar added that anyone who caused this by mistake shall be punished by imprisonment for a period of not less than three months, and a fine of not less than fifty thousand pounds and not exceeding two hundred thousand pounds, or one of these two penalties. If the crime occurred on an information network belonging to the state or a public legal person or If it is owned or managed by it, the penalty shall be aggravated imprisonment and a fine of not less than five hundred thousand pounds and not exceeding one million pounds.

Mukhtar pointed out that the law also established the penalty of aggravated imprisonment if a crime is committed that exposes the security and safety of society to danger, and the text of Article 34 states: “If any of the crimes stipulated in this law is committed for the purpose of disrupting public order or exposing the safety and security of society to danger or harming national security.” of the country or its economic position, or preventing or obstructing the public authorities from carrying out their work, or disrupting the provisions of the Constitution, laws, or regulations, or harming national unity and social peace, the penalty shall be aggravated imprisonment.

It is noteworthy that the law stipulates a number of penalties for anyone who creates a private account or website through which he aims to hinder the authorities from their work or to spread lies, as he shall be punished by imprisonment for a period of not less than two years and a fine of not less than 100 thousand pounds and not more than 300 thousand, or one of the following: These two penalties apply to anyone who creates, manages, or uses a website or a private account on an information network with the aim of committing or facilitating the commission of a crime punishable by law.
